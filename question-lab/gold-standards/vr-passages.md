## Passage 1

As a child, Manolo Doñate often saw strange men with dogs in the mountains near his house in Sarrión, a town in the Teruel province of Aragón in Spain. They were hunting the abundant wild truffles. In the 1980s, while visiting a plantation in France, he decided to become the first in Teruel to cultivate truffle-producing oak trees. "People thought he was crazy," says Simona, his daughter. Little was known about how to farm the fungus; it takes around 10 years for the trees to produce. But it worked. Today Teruel is the world's biggest producer of Tuber melanosporum, the prince of black truffles. Last year the region exported more than 26,000 kg of them. Production from France's Périgord region, previously the biggest producer, has plummeted over the past century, as farmers switched to easier crops. Truffle farmers must introduce the fungus to young trees and replant each tree in a field; when the truffles produce, finding them requires trained animals. But Teruel's meagre soil is unfit for most crops, so locals have bet everything on the black diamonds. Production has enriched farmers: black truffles fetch up to €700 per kilogram. And it has halted depopulation: Sarrión's population has held steady even as Aragón's has fallen. "The next step is international recognition," says Daniel Brito, head of Teruel's truffle-farming association. Périgord truffles are still the gold standard. Mr Brito hopes to win a 'geographical indication', like those for champagne or Parma ham, before the next season starts. Another challenge is that Spaniards rarely eat truffles. They were banned during the Spanish Inquisition (1478 to 1834 AD) as a poison from hell. To Teruel, however, they are a gift from heaven.

**Q1. According to the passage, Teruel is known for:**
A. the notable quality of its soil.
B. the increasing consumption of truffles.
C. its high production of black truffles.
D. the premium price of its truffles.

Correct response: C
Option C is correct because paragraph 2 says, "Today Teruel is the world's biggest producer of Tuber melanosporum, the prince of black truffles."

**Q2. Which of the following statements is best supported by information in the passage?**
A. Spanish truffle farmers are wealthier than French truffle farmers.
B. Farmed black truffles were originally harvested in Teruel in the late twentieth century.
C. Truffles are a successful crop because they are easy to harvest.
D. French farmers switched to easier crops because the Périgord truffles were no longer in demand.

Correct response: B
Option B is correct because paragraph 1 states that Manolo Doñate "in the 1980s … decided to become the first in Teruel to cultivate truffle-producing oak trees". Paragraph 2 states that it takes around 10 years for the trees to produce and that "it worked".

**Q3. Each of the following statements can be supported by information in the passage except:**
A. Teruel has always had many truffles in the region.
B. truffle cultivation is labour intensive.
C. Aragón is a mountainous region.
D. locals like to eat truffles for their supper.

Correct response: D
Option D is correct because it is the only response that is not supported by the text. According to paragraph 4, "...Spaniards rarely eat truffles. They were banned during the Spanish Inquisition (1478 to 1834 AD) as a poison from hell."

**Q4. According to the passage, Spaniards rarely eat truffles because:**
A. historically they were a forbidden food.
B. they are all sold to other countries.
C. they do not yet have international recognition.
D. people prefer to eat Parma ham.

Correct response: A
Option A is correct because paragraph 4 states that truffles "were banned during the Spanish Inquisition (1478 to 1834 AD) as a poison from hell".

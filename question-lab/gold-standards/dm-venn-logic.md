# DM Venn Logic — Official UCAT examples

---

50 students were asked which types of cultural visits they had made in the last year: Cinema, Theatre, Museum. All students had made at least one visit. No student had been to both the Theatre and the Cinema. 10 students had been to the Cinema and the Museum. Twice as many had been to the Theatre and the Museum. 9 students had been to just the Museum. 7 students had been to just the Cinema.

How many more students had made a visit to the Museum than had made a visit to the Theatre?

A. 5
B. 15
C. 19
D. 25

Correct: B

The number who made a visit to the Museum is 39. The number who made a visit to the Theatre is 24. The difference is 15 more.

---

A group of people were asked how they like their eggs cooked. 15 said boiled, 12 said scrambled and 13 said fried. 4 said they liked exactly two of the ways and 7 said they liked all three ways. 1 person said they hated eggs.

How many people were asked?

A. 17
B. 19
C. 21
D. 23

Correct: D

15 − 7 = 8 like boiled only or boiled + one other. 12 − 7 = 5 like scrambled only or scrambled + one other. 13 − 7 = 6 like fried only or fried + one other. 8 + 5 + 6 = 19 like only one or two kinds. 19 − 4 = 15 like only one kind. Total = 15 + 7 + 1 = 23.

Here is some information about the methods some students used to keep fit: Cycling, Swimming, Running. No student used all three methods. 16 students used exactly two methods. 13 students used only Running. 12 students used both Cycling and Swimming.

How many students used Running to keep fit?

A. 17
B. 25
C. 29
D. 41

Correct: A

r + q + 12 = 16, so r + q = 4. Number running = 13 + r + q = 13 + 4 = 17.

---

There are 48 people in a room. They are asked what they like doing in their spare time. 29 of the people say they like reading. 9 of the people say they like watching television.

Which of the following MUST be true?

A. Between 10 and 19 (inclusive) people neither like reading nor like watching television.
B. There are exactly 20 people who like reading but do not like watching television.
C. There are more people who do not like reading than who do not like watching television.
D. There are 38 people who like either reading or watching television.

Correct: A

38 people (29 + 9) like reading and/or watching television. The range of people who like neither: minimum = 48 − 38 = 10 (if no overlap); maximum = 48 − 29 = 19 (if all TV-watchers also read). B is wrong because overlap is unknown. C is wrong: 19 don't like reading vs 39 don't like TV. D is wrong because overlap is unknown.

---

In Daleham, half of the 320 houses have both a front garden and a back garden. 100 houses don't have a back garden, and 125 houses don't have a front garden.

How many houses have neither a front garden nor a back garden?

A. 25
B. 50
C. 65
D. 95

Correct: C

160 houses have both gardens. Let x = neither. Houses with only front = 100 − x; houses with only back = 125 − x. (100 − x) + 160 + (125 − x) + x = 320 → 385 − x = 320 → x = 65.

---

Of the 150 aquariums across a US state, 60 had yellow crabs, 43 had white dolphins, 81 had starfish, 20 had only starfish and white dolphins, 12 had only white dolphins and yellow crabs, 9 had only starfish and yellow crabs, and 7 had all three.

How many aquariums had none of these three?

A. 19
B. 21
C. 27
D. 32

Correct: B

Total in Venn = (60 − 12 − 9 − 7) + (43 − 20 − 12 − 7) + (81 − 20 − 9 − 7) + 12 + 20 + 9 + 7 = 32 + 4 + 45 + 48 = 129. Aquariums with none = 150 − 129 = 21.

---

There are 40 people in a library. 24 have mobile phones and 18 have laptops. 8 have neither a mobile nor a laptop.

How many have both?

A. 10
B. 12
C. 14
D. 16

Correct: A

40 − 8 = 32 have at least one. Let x = both. (24 − x) + x + (18 − x) = 32 → 42 − x = 32 → x = 10.

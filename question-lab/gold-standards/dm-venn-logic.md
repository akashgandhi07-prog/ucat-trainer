15 said boiled, 12 said scrambled and 13 said fried.

Correct response: D 

 

From the information given, we can work out

15 (people who like boiled eggs) - 7 (people who like all three kinds of eggs) = 8 people like only boiled eggs or boiled eggs and only one other

12 (people who like scrambled eggs) - 7 (people who like all three kinds of eggs) = 5 (people who like only scrambled eggs or scrambled and only one other)

13 (people who like fried eggs) - 7 (people who like all three kinds of eggs) = 6 (people who like only fried eggs or fried and only one other)

Adding together, we get 8 + 5 + 6 = 19 people like only one kind of egg

So 19 - 4 = 15 people like only one kind or only two kinds

 

Total number of people who were asked = 15 + 7 +1 = 23

Correct response: D

 

D is correct because suppose the flavours are A, B and C. Initially the number of flavour combinations could be A, B, C, AB, BC, AC and ABC (7 flavours). If we introduce a new flavour D, the new combinations (up to 3 flavours) would be D, DA, DB, DC, DAC, DAB and DBC = 7.

A, B, C give 3 new combinations, AB, AC, BC give 3 new combinations, plus the new flavour itself.

There are 48 people in a room. They are asked what they like doing in their spare time.

 

29 of the people say they like reading.

9 of the people say they like watching television. 

 

Which of the following MUST be true?


A.

Between 10 and 19 (inclusive) people neither like reading nor like watching television.


A.

Between 10 and 19 (inclusive) people neither like reading nor like watching television.


B.

There are exactly 20 people who like reading but do not like watching television.


B.

There are exactly 20 people who like reading but do not like watching television.


C.

There are more people who do not like reading than who do not like watching television.


C.

There are more people who do not like reading than who do not like watching television.


D.

There are 38 people who like either reading or watching television.


D.

There are 38 people who like either reading or watching television.

Correct response: A

 

A is correct because in total, 38 people (29 + 9) like reading and/or watching television. We don't know if there is any overlap between those who like reading and those who like watching television, so the range of people who neither like reading nor like watching television must be as follows:

 

Total number of people (48) − total number of people who like reading and/or watching television (38) = 10

Total number of people (48) − minimum number of people who like only reading (29) = 19

 

B is incorrect because we don't know if there is any overlap between those who like reading and those who like watching television.

C is incorrect because 29 people like reading. Therefore, 48 − 29 = 19 people do not like reading. 9 people like watching television. Therefore, 48 − 9 = 39 people do not like watching television. More people do not like watching television than do not like reading. 

D is incorrect because we don't know if there is any overlap between those who like reading and those who like watching television.

Correct response: C

 

C is correct because 160 houses have two gardens (320 / 2 = 160).

x (houses with neither a front nor a back garden) can be worked out as follows:

100 – x is the number of houses with only a front garden and 125 – x is the number of houses with only a back garden

100 – x + 160 + 125 – x + x = 320

385 – 320 = x

x = 65
